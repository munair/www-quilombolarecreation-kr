Beginner Classes

Join us on Friday evenings at 8:30 PM for Capoeira fundamentals. In these sessions we train fundamental kicks and escapes, magical ginga steps, and a few other basic movements essential to expressing yourself in a Capoeira game. These movements look easy, but they are truly difficult to master. Mastery can take years, but no matter how long the journey takes, training Capoeira is always a fun and challenging workout filled with activities that make great and lasting friendships.

The class is taught to a cohort. The cohort size is 10 people. The class runs for one month. You can signup again and again. There is no limit to the number of times you can signup for this course.

Intermediate Classes

Join us Monday through Thursday at 8:30 PM for regular Capoeira classes. In these evening sessions we train sequencies and work with partners to improve our Capoeira vocabulary and reflexes.

Attendance Restrictions
Class open to intermediate and advanced students only (i.e. you need to be "baptized" or have at least one year of capoeira experience under your belt before you can attend these classes.)

Advanced Classes

Join us weekends at 11:00 AM for advanced Capoeira training. In these mid-morning sessions we train advanced capoeira movement and concepts.

Attendance Restrictions
Class open to advanced students only. You need approval from the teacher to be able to attend these sessions. Contact Instrutor Zumbi via Facebook or call him on 010-7293-1777 to find out if you can attend.

Workshops

Workshops are open to all ability levels and usually end with a berimbau-led "roda" and fellowship over a meal.



Teachers and Assistants

Instrutor Zumbi

Instrutor Zumbi is the founder of the Cordão de Ouro Capoeira group in Seoul and principal instructor at Quilombola Recreation. He began teaching Capoeira in San Francisco and Emeryville, California in 2005. Zumbi started learning Capoeira in September 2000 while studying at the University of California at Berkeley. Zumbi moved to Korea to work for SK Telecom in the New Media division and led classes in Korea since 2009 in his spare time. In 2012, Zumbi retired from SK and launched Quilombola Recreation via Korea's foreign direct investment scheme to continue his teaching mission.

King

King started learning Capoeira in 2011 and quickly mastered all of Instrutor Zumbi's classes. He received his yellow cord in Paris, France from Mestre Chicote (Cordão de Ouro) in May 2013 and has been authorized to assist Zumbi in teaching classes since then. Under Zumbi's direction, King teaches classes in Korean and is responsible for maintaining Cordão de Ouro Seoul's Korean language blog.

Tigresa

Tigresa started learning Capoeira in 2012 and has rearly missed any of Instrutor Zumbi's classes.
